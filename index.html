Typography

The Department of the Interior uses the following three typefaces:

Ubuntu as Display Font 1

AaBbCcDdEeFfGgHhIiJjKkLl MmNnOoPpQqRrSsTtUuVv WwXxYyZz 123456789!@#$%

We dug through the online world for the perfect typeface for our headings and found Ubuntu, as it is brings us into the 21st century and emphasizes credibility with our organization and its’ priorities.

Raleway Semi-Bold as Display Font 2

AaBbCcDdEeFfGgHhIiJjKkLl MmNnOoPpQqRrSsTtUuVv WwXxYyZz 123456789!@#$%

Raleway Semi-Bold is a typeface which is fun to read and reminiscent of signage from the railway and natural forest preserves. This is a perfect attention-grabbing font, particulary for blogtitles.

Open Sans Light as Body Text

AaBbCcDdEeFfGgHhIiJjKkLl MmNnOoPpQqRrSsTtUuVv WwXxYyZz 123456789!@#$%

Open Sans is the perfect body text as it compliments both Ubuntu and Raleway. It will be easy to ready on our online platforms, and it is both friendly and confident.

Simple & Clean Iconography

Iconography is used throughout the site to navigate and inform the user. Aesthetic is clean, simple, with rounded edges.

Imagery

Optimistic, beautiful, positive, fun, open-minded, environmental, vibrant, diverse

Images should include nature, appear adventurous, and highlight the beauty of the variety of landscapes and people that belong to the United States of America. Landscape, nature, documentary-style and aerial shots will be used primarily to demonstrate the concept and style of the Department of the Interior.
